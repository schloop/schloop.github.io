Traveling around Germany

Frankfurt is centrally located so that many popular destinations are easily accessible via train. From the Frankfurt Main Station, you can easily reach cities such as Cologne, Munich, Nuremberg, Berlin, Hamburg, Strasburg, Paris, Brussels, Amsterdam, or Prague. Here is a website that explains how the train system works in Germany. It includes useful information for when it is best to buy tickets, what kinds of trains there are, how to identify the train you need, and where to buy tickets. Please read this if you plan on taking any type of train in Germany. Tip for traveling throughout Europe: we always find it useful to download offline maps of the city from Google Maps to find our way around in foreign cities. Here's a how-to.

Traveling to the wedding

Giessen is located approximately 43 miles north of Frankfurt. We recommend taking the train to get there, as it is easy and quick—approximately 1 hour from the Frankfurt Main Station. You can buy train tickets either on the Deutsche Bahn ("German train") website, the app, "DB Navigator" (available for both iOS and Android), or at any of the blue-green kiosks in the train station. You can find the appropriate trains and times by entering "Frankfurt(Main)Hbf" and "Giessen" as your destinations. There are several different trains that can take you there and they run often throughout the day. Use the DB app or website to find the appropriate times.


Your stop will be announced in the train in German, and it is simply called "Giessen" (pronounced geese-in). There will also be scrolling signs in the train listing the upcoming stops, and you can always see the name of the station outside when you arrive. I always like to pay attention to the estimated arrival time listed on my ticket if I ever lose track of the stops. It is well possible that a train may leave the Frankfurt station late (sometimes even up to 20-30 minutes), so be sure to allow plenty of time to make your way to the church if you plan on arriving shortly before the wedding starts. If you use the DP app, it provides real-time departure and arrival times. If you wish to arrive in Giessen early the day of the wedding and need to kill time, you will see several informal dining options to grab a bite to eat on the way, either within the train station or on the way to the church.

Our Suggestions

Here are some of our personal suggestions for the sights to see in Frankfurt, our favorite restaurants, cafes, and places to go, and our favorite spots to travel around the region and in Germany. We ordered the list by how highly we recommend it.

Traveling around Frankfurt is often best done using the subway sytem: the U-bahn. For most sightseeing destinations, you could get away with walking if you're comfortable with it. The distance between each U-bahn stop in the city-center equals about a 10-minute walk. Tickets for all public transport can be bought at the blue-green kiosks in the stations and are valid for busses or U-bahns. For a single person to take public transportation, it’s cheapest to buy a day ticket (around 6 euros) if you plan on taking it more than once in a day. For people traveling together, it is cheaper to buy a group ticket for groups up to 5 people. If you choose this option, you must write the names of each person that will be included in this ticket on the backside before you get on the subway. If you do not do this, the ticket is not valid. (Caution: "student" tickets are only valid for German high schoolers, so do not choose this option even if you still have your college ID.) You do not need to validate the ticket electronically. Train guards will hop on and off for random checks. If you are caught with an invalid ticket, you will be charged a 60 euro fine. They will not be lenient even for confused out-of-towners and often do not speak English well, if at all. You can pick up a transportation map and more ticketing information at Hauptwache (the subway station at the city center, right where Zeil and Galeria Kaufhof are): either at the round "traffiQ" kiosk above ground or in the VGF office under ground.

What to do in Frankfurt

Sightseeing
Römerplatz: "Roman square" *rec
Neue Altstadt: "New old town” *rec
Dom: cathedral
Alte Oper: "old opera house" *rec
Fressgass': "snacking street", street leading to Alte Oper with many cafés and restaurants *rec
Zeil: shopping street, city center
Galeria Kaufhof SkyBar: great for a rooftop beer/wine with a panoramic view *fav *rec
Kleinmarkthalle: indoor farmer's market, and on Wednesdays and Saturdays there's an outdoor market just behind the building *fav
Westend Campus: Sarah's university with a nice beer garden, "Sommergarten" (U-Bahn stop: Holzhausenstraße) *fav
Mainkai: refreshing riverside stroll along the Main in the summer
Judengasse: former Jewish ghetto from 1462-1796 with the original brick wall gate, found on the street now called "Fahrgasse"
Paulskirche: "St. Paul's Church"
MainTower: birdseye view over Frankfurt region *rec
Palmengarten: large indoor/outdoor botanical garden

Restaurants

Zum Lahmen Esel: traditional German food, specifically from this region, try the applewine! *fav *rec
Gaststätte Atschel: traditional German food, also specifically from the region
Hans im Glück: best burger in town *fav
Buster Pasta: affordable, pasta and pizza
Naiv: craft beer bar *fav
RamenJun: ramen bar
Aroydee Thai: affordable, Thai

Cafés
Bohnerie: our go-to café, terrific home-roasted coffee, great cakes *fav
Bitter & Zart: best cake, good coffee *fav *rec
Tachlesz Bistro: good coffee and cake, nice city view from upstairs seating area *rec
Holy Cross Brewing Society: specialty coffee, good pastries

Where to go in Germany

Marburg: traditional, picturesque German-looking "small" town with cathedral, hilltop castle/museum, right near Giessen *fav *rec
Munich: Sarah's favorite city to visit, home to Oktoberfest with many great breweries and traditional German food, famous Residenz castle/museum, many beautiful churches and town squares *fav *rec
Hamburg: lively, modern port city with different German culture of the North (Daniel really likes it here!)
Mainz: German-looking city near Frankfurt, situated along the river in popular wine region with pretty cathedral *fav
Heidelberg: popular tourist destination, has a castle and picturesque old town
Cologne: famous cathedral and the home of Kölsch beer
Berlin: trendy city with lots of historic spots and wide variety of things to do
